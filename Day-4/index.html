Certainly! Here's a fun fact about the Roman Empire:

The ancient Romans were avid users of a condiment called "garum." Garum was a fermented fish sauce that served as a popular and pungent flavor enhancer in Roman cuisine. It was made by fermenting fish, typically small fish like anchovies, in salt for several weeks or months. The resulting sauce was then used in various dishes to add a savory and umami flavor. Garum was so widely used that it became a staple in Roman cooking, and archaeological evidence of garum production facilities has been found throughout the former Roman Empire.
